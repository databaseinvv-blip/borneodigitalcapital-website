Borneo Digital Capital launches a RM100 million Sarawak digital-infrastructure fund.

Kuala Lumpur-based BDG PLT is established to deploy long-horizon capital into Sarawak's data-centre, connectivity, and future-economy sectors through the 2030 development cycle.

Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia — Borneo Digital Capital (BDG) has been established as a Sarawak-focused private equity fund built to invest exclusively in the state's fast-growing digital economy. The fund targets RM100 million in commitments, with a first close of RM10 million, and is managed by Aureus Strategies PE Sdn Bhd, a licensed private equity company operating under the Securities Commission Malaysia framework.

BDG deploys capital across three complementary strategies: a defensive, core-yield digital-infrastructure book; a structured-growth allocation to smart-city and AI platforms; and a future-economy sleeve spanning cloud, fintech, and digital-enablement businesses. The design pairs contracted, yield-based cashflow with selective exposure to the state's highest-growth digital opportunities — capital preservation first, yield enhancement second.

The mandate is anchored in the Sarawak Development Strategy 2030 (SDS 2030), the state's published economic blueprint, which targets a doubling of state GDP to RM282 billion by the end of the decade — with digital infrastructure named an explicit priority sector.

Investment thesis

BDG's central view is structural. Sarawak's abundant, low-cost renewable energy is becoming the foundation for the digital infrastructure of the next economy — a conversion the firm describes as the move from Watt to Byte. Cheap, clean, reliable power is the primary input to data centres and the compute-intensive industries that cluster around them, giving the state a durable cost advantage that few jurisdictions in Southeast Asia can match at scale.

Capital that understands the place will compound differently than capital that treats Sarawak as one option among many.

Advisory bench

BDG is supported by a senior advisory bench drawn from institutional capital markets across Hong Kong and Asia, including Derek Cheng, Senior Advisor at the Blackstone Group and former CEO and Managing Director of China Resources Capital, and Lewis Wan, Chairman of the Hong Kong Financial Asset Exchange Group. The firm's General Partners combine licensed financial advisory experience, Hong Kong-regulated asset management, M&A advisory across hundreds of transactions, and direct operating experience in Sarawak's digital and infrastructure sectors.

Where the fund invests

  • ◆Digital Infrastructure — data-centre facilities, fibre, and the connectivity backbone, backed by concession or availability contracts.
  • ◆Smart City & AI — intelligent systems, IoT deployments, and public-sector digital service concessions.
  • ◆Digital Enablement — cloud, SaaS, and fintech platforms on recurring-revenue models.
  • ◆Green Data Power — renewable-backed, sustainable compute anchored in Sarawak's clean-energy advantage.
